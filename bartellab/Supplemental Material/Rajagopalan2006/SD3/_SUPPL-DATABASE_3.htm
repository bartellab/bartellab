Supplementary Database 3.  Small RNAs mapping to Arabidopsis TAS loci
Each tasiRNA locus listed in blue is hyperlinked to an image (in GIF format) with two graphics.  The first is a radial graph whose spokes each represent one of the 21 possible phasing registers.  The total number of small RNAs whose 5' end corresponds to a particular register is plotted as distance from the center of the graph along that register's spoke.  The register specified by the miRNA cleavage site is indicated with a grey circle. The percentage of small RNAs mapping to the locus that fall within the miRNA-specified register is given. The second image in the file is a plot of the abundance profile of reads at each position on the annotated TAS sequence, given as the absolute count of reads whose 5'-most nucleotide is at the indicated position.  Datapoints above the axis represent sense reads, while those below the axis represent reads to the antisense strand.  The miRNA interaction site is indicated by a red bar, and the cleavage site by an arrow.  
The "alignment of reads" link is hyperlinked to alignments of all small RNA sequences to that particular TAS locus. The sequence of the locus is given followed by all matching small RNA sequences anchored to it, along with the start (Start) and end (End) indices of the match on the locus, the read frequency (Freq) and number of genomic hits (Hits) for each small RNA. Bracketed species represent small RNAs that mapped to the antisense strand of the locus.
