Supplementary Database 1.  Small RNAs mapping to previously annotated miRNA loci
This file contains updates to the "Previously annotated miRBase sequence" columns and the "Revised sequence" column.  It only includes information on the previously annotated sequence if that sequence differs from the most abundant uniquely-mapping miRNA sequence in the dataset.  It only includes a revised sequence if the most abundant sequence agrees with data on 5' RACE-verified targets for the miRNA in the literature, or if no RACE data has been reported for the miRNA's targets.  The revised sequences have been submitted to miRBase.
A tab-delimited text file of small RNA sequences in DNA form, with the indices of match to hairpins, can be accessed here.
Each miRNA locus listed in blue was represented by reads in the dataset and is hyperlinked to an image (in GIF format) illustrating the predicted hairpin deriving from that locus in DNA form.  The miRNA sequence is rendered in red and the most abundant sequence on the opposite arm is in green.  Nucleotides 5' of the miRNA are in black, nucleotides 3' of the miRNA are in blue.   Also depicted is the abundance profile of reads at each position on the hairpin, given as the absolute count of reads whose 5'-most nucleotide is at the indicated position, and whose 3'-most nucleotide is at the indicated position.  Datapoints above the axis represent sense reads, while those below the axis represent reads to the antisense strand.
The "reads" link in the "Alignment" column is hyperlinked to alignments of all small RNA sequences to previously annotated miRNA hairpins. The sequence of the miRNA hairpin is shown above its predicted secondary structure in bracket notation as determined by RNAFold.  The alignment of the annotated mature miRNA is illustrated in red below the structure depiction.  This is followed by all matching small RNA sequences anchored to the hairpin sequence, along with the read frequency (Freq) and number of genomic hits (Hits) for each small RNA. Bracketed species represent small RNAs that mapped to the antisense strand of the locus.
Sequence of the most abundant miRNA variant mapping to the hairpin is given in bold black text.
