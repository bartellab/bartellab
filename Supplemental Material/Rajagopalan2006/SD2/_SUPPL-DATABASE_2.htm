Supplementary Database 2.  Small RNAs mapping to newly identified miRNA loci
A tab-delimited text file of small RNA sequences in DNA form, with the indices of match to hairpins, can be accessed here.
Each miRNA name is hyperlinked to an image (in GIF format) illustrating the predicted hairpin in DNA form.  The miRNA sequence is rendered in red and the most abundant sequence on the opposite arm is in green.  Nucleotides 5' of the miRNA are in black, nucleotides 3' of the miRNA are in blue.   Also depicted is the abundance profile of reads at each position on the hairpin, given as the absolute count of reads whose 5'-most nucleotide is at the indicated position, and whose 3'-most nucleotide is at the indicated position.  Datapoints above the axis represent sense reads, while those below the axis represent reads to the antisense strand.
The "reads" link in the "Alignment" column is hyperlinked to alignments of all small RNA sequences to newly identified miRNA hairpins. The sequence of the miRNA hairpin is shown above its predicted secondary structure in bracket notation as determined by RNAFold.  The alignment of the mature miRNA is illustrated in red below the structure depiction.  This is followed by all matching small RNA sequences anchored to the hairpin sequence, along with the read frequency (Freq) and number of genomic hits (Hits) for each small RNA. Bracketed species represent small RNAs that mapped to the antisense strand of the locus.
